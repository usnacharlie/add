Our History

The Alliance for Democracy and Development (ADD) was founded on May 14, 2010 by Charles Milupi, an independent Member of Parliament who envisioned a democratic and development-focused political movement in Zambia that champions entrepreneurship and wealth creation.

Key Milestones

May 2010
Party Formation

ADD officially founded by Charles Milupi with a vision for democratic governance, national development, and wealth creation through entrepreneurship.

2011
First Elections

Charles Milupi contested as presidential candidate, finishing 4th with 0.9% of the vote. ADD won 1 National Assembly seat in Luena constituency (Mwambwa Imenda).

2018
Opposition Alliance

ADD joined the Opposition Alliance, demonstrating commitment to collaborative politics and democratic unity.

February 2021
UPND Alliance Leadership

Opposition Alliance rebranded as UPND Alliance with Charles Milupi as Chairperson, endorsing Hakainde Hichilema for President.

August 2021
UPND Alliance Government

Following UPND Alliance's electoral victory, Charles Milupi appointed as Minister of Infrastructure, Housing and Urban Development, demonstrating ADD's key role as a UPND Alliance partner.

UPND Alliance Partner

ADD is a proud partner in the UPND Alliance, the ruling political alliance in Zambia since August 2021. Through this partnership, we continue to advance our vision of democracy, development, and wealth creation for all Zambians.

Alliance Partnership

Working together with UPND to deliver on our shared vision for Zambia's future and economic transformation.

Government Role

ADD President Charles Milupi serves as Minister of Infrastructure, Housing and Urban Development.
